# ShadowBlade · Voice & Tone

## Voice profile

**Confident, plainspoken, never breathless.** ShadowBlade sounds like the senior PM in the room who has shipped this before: certain of the outcome, generous with the proof, allergic to theater. We lead with the customer's result, then show the mechanism. We don't hype, we don't hedge, and we never reach for a synonym when the plain word will do.

We write for an enterprise reader who already owns a render pipeline of human contractors and now wants their Tuesday back. Every sentence should pass the "would a head of brand say this on stage without flinching?" test.

## Pillars

| Pillar | Means | Sounds like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Outcome-first | Lead with the result the buyer can name in their next standup. | "Ship a customer story in the time it used to take to brief one." |
| Specific over clever | Cite numbers, formats, and seconds. Never vague verbs. | "4-minute first cut, 92% reviewer approval, $168k saved per quarter." |
| Quiet authority | Calm declaratives. No exclamation marks, no buzzwords. | "Renders are on schedule. Drift is auto-resolved before review." |
| Operator-grade | Show that we know how production actually breaks. | "Compose holds on overlay #3. Rush jobs jump the queue. You'll see it." |

## Do / Avoid

| Do | Avoid |
| --- | --- |
| Lead with the customer outcome | Lead with the feature name |
| Use single-syllable verbs (ship, cut, run, save) | "Empower", "leverage", "unlock", "revolutionize" |
| Cap on-screen lines at 14 words | Run-on sentences that need two readings |
| Name the metric and the unit | "Significantly faster" / "10x better" with no baseline |
| One idea per sentence | Stacking three claims joined by commas |
| End on a verb of action | End on an emotion ("…and we're so excited!") |
| Lower-case sentence headlines | TITLE CASE EVERY MARKETING CARD |
| Plain product names (Studio, Queue, Brand kit) | Codename theater (Project Aurora, Hydra-Suite) |
| Numbers as digits ("4 minutes", "92%") | Numbers spelled out in copy |
| Trust signals quietly footed | Trust signals as the headline |

## One-liners (drop-in copy, ≤ 14 words each)

1. From brief to broadcast-ready video in four minutes.
2. The video pipeline marketing, training, and product teams run their factory on.
3. Brand-locked output. Every cut, every render, every region.
4. Ship a customer story in the time it used to take to brief one.
5. Four-minute first cuts. Ninety-two percent approval. Audit log included.
6. The enterprise video cloud built for the people who own the calendar.

## Don't ship if…

- A sentence needs a thesaurus to read.
- The claim has no number behind it.
- The headline says "AI" before it says what the buyer gets.
- Anything ends in an exclamation mark.
- A noun has been verbed ("we *workflow* the team").

## Tone-shift cheatsheet

| Surface | Tone dial |
| --- | --- |
| Landing hero | Confident, declarative, one claim |
| Dashboard empty states | Helpful operator ("Drop a brief here to start.") |
| Pipeline error | Calm, specific, fixable ("Overlay #3 missing. Re-link or auto-pick.") |
| Approval emails | Brisk, named, one CTA |
| Changelog | Engineer-to-engineer, present tense, no marketing |
| Customer quotes | Pull the verb, not the adjective ("We ship 14× more cuts.") |
